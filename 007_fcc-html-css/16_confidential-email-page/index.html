Confidential

To FBI Special Agent Fox Mulder

Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), now often referred to as Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) by government and scientific bodies, have captivated the public imagination since the modern wave of sightings began in the post-World War II era.

The phenomenon gained widespread attention in 1947 with Kenneth Arnold’s famous "flying saucer" report, which led to a surge in public interest and subsequent military investigations like the U.S. Air Force’s Project Blue Book. Although the majority of these thousands of historical sightings were eventually attributed to misidentified aircraft, weather phenomena, or hoaxes, a small percentage of credible reports from military personnel and reliable observers remained unexplained.

This persistent core of unexplained events has fueled decades of speculation, ranging from secret government technology to the popular, yet unsubstantiated, extraterrestrial hypothesis
